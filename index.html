The first picture is a 'Lesser Bougaivillea', which is the most common species of bougainvillea used for bonsai. The next one to the right, on top, is 'Aleurites', which is a small genus of arborescent flowering plants in the Euphorbiaceae. The one right under is 'Nosegaytree', but it has pink and white petals. The middle picture is Indian Banyan that is native to the Indian Subcontinent. The next one on the top is the 'Sleeping Grass' because when you touch them, their leaves close. The one under that one is 'Ohi'a Lehua, and it is endemic to Hawai'i. The last photo on the top Be-Still Tree that is poisonous throught out New Mexico. The one on the bottom is the Bauhinia that is named after 2 Swiss-French brothers.

iNaturalist is a nature app where you use your camera device to take pictures of plants, animals or even fungi that you do not know anything about!

The app helps you identify what you take pictures of, by giving similar pictures taken by other people and you can choose which one looks visually similar. Once you've found the closest one, you can click on it and find a little more information about that certain species and seven see more pictures.

As a user, iNaturalist is better than any other identifying app out because it allows you to take pictures of animals and fungi as well. I have used other apps and they were very disappointing because they did not give me the option to take pictures of other animals and fungi. I would definetely recommend iNaturalist to others because it is super easy to use and it can help people be more aware of their surroundings because alot of people do not know what surrounding them!

My picture is of different plants around Kapiolani Communtiy College that I walk past everyday but always wondered what they were. Through iNaturalist, I was able to identify almost all of the plants I was looking for because other people were wondering about the same plants as me.
